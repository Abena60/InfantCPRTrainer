    Drowning is the second leading cause of death for children between the ages of 5 and 14, and it is the leading cause of death for children between the ages of 1 and 4. Tragic as it is, this issue is LARGELY preventable. Parents must realize that they are their child's sole protector. Water is a valuable resource that we need to survive, but it is also dangerous. You must always watch a child when they are near or around water, including the times when they are not even swimming, because one minute is all it takes for an infant to drown.

    That being said, we are only human and mistakes happen all the time. That is why it is of upmost importance for all child caregivers to be knowledgeble in infant CPR, a simple yet crucial skill that can keep someone alive despite all odds.
